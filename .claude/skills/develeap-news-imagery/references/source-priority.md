# Source priority — pick a tier, don't gamble

Walk these in order. Use the first tier whose **legality bar** is fully cleared. If any doubt, drop to the next tier. Tier 4 (AI) is always safe — falling through is acceptable, guessing on licensing is not.

## Tier 1 — Real press image (newsroom-issued)

The article is about a specific product launch / company announcement / policy ruling, AND the company or government body has published a press image *explicitly licensed for editorial reuse*.

**Use when:**
- Vendor newsroom links to a "press kit" / "media assets" page with explicit terms
- The page says one of: "free for editorial use", "may be reproduced", "press use permitted"
- The image is hosted on the vendor's own domain (`*.aws.amazon.com/press/`, `news.microsoft.com`, etc.)
- Government bodies (CISA, NIST, ENISA) publish public-domain or CC0 images on their official sites

**Reject when:**
- License terms are missing, ambiguous, or "all rights reserved"
- Image is from a third-party news outlet (those are licensed *to that outlet*, not to Develeap)
- Watermarked, has an embedded credit bar, or an obvious stock-house source (Getty, Shutterstock, Reuters)
- Page says "for journalists only" without spelling out reuse rights

**Record:** `image_url`, `attribution` (e.g. "Image: AWS Press Kit"), `license_note` (paste the license sentence verbatim).

## Tier 2 — Event photo (conferences, launches, official photo ops)

The article references a concrete event with an official photographer, AND the photo is published under an explicit reuse license.

**Use when:**
- AWS re:Invent / KubeCon / Microsoft Build / Google I/O / OpenAI DevDay official Flickr or media pool with CC license
- Official government press conference photos (e.g., White House photographer, public domain)
- The license is CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, CC0, or public domain — and you can attribute correctly

**Reject when:**
- License is CC-BY-NC (we're a corporate site, "non-commercial" doesn't apply cleanly)
- License is CC-BY-ND (no derivatives — fine for hot-link, awkward for layout crops)
- Source is a news photographer's portfolio without explicit license

**Record:** `image_url`, `attribution` (e.g. "Photo: Linux Foundation / KubeCon NA 2025, CC-BY"), `license_note`.

## Tier 3 — Brand logo on tinted background (compositional intent only)

The article is *about* a specific company or product with a recognizable logo, AND no Tier 1/2 image is available, AND the logo's mark is published on a press/brand page.

**This tier produces a *layout intent*, not an image file.** The skill records:
- The brand color (from `brand-colors.md` or the brand's official guidelines)
- A pointer to the logo asset (URL on the brand's domain)
- A short layout note ("logo centered, 30% width, on solid #326CE5 background, 16:9")

The consumer (Yair / WordPress / Figma) composes the final asset. The skill does not generate the composite, because the consumer may want SVG / React / static PNG depending on surface.

**Use when:**
- Article is genuinely about *one* identifiable brand (Kubernetes 1.31, Stripe outage, AWS Lambda update)
- The brand publishes a logo in a brand-resources / press-kit page

**Reject when:**
- The article touches multiple brands (Tier 4 is cleaner than mashing logos)
- Brand explicitly forbids logo display on tinted backgrounds (rare, but check)

**Record:** `source_type: "brand_logo_bg"`, `lead_color`, a short `attribution` ("Kubernetes logo © CNCF"), `image_url: null`, `image_path: null`.

## Tier 4 — AI generation via Pollinations Flux (default fallback)

Always available, always free, always Develeap-owned. This is the catch-all.

**Use when:**
- None of Tiers 1-3 are cleared
- The article is conceptual ("the rise of agentic AI", "open-source governance debate") — no specific image fits
- The publisher (HN, blog post) doesn't ship a press image
- We're processing a batch and don't have time to vet 17 different licenses

**The recipe is locked** — see SKILL.md for the GET URL and `scripts/generate_images.py` for the implementation. The subject sentence is written per `prompt-recipes.md` and `composition-discipline.md`.

**Record:** `source_type: "ai_pollinations_flux"`, `image_path` (local file written by the script), `prompt_used`, `lead_color`, `composition`, `license_note: "Generated by Pollinations.ai (Flux model). Develeap holds usage rights."`, `attribution: null`.

## Quick decision examples

| Article | Best tier | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "AWS re:Invent 2025 keynote announces…" | Tier 2 (event photo) → Tier 3 (AWS logo on orange) → Tier 4 | Official re:Invent photos exist with CC license. If not findable in 30s, drop to logo. |
| "Kubernetes 1.31 ships with sidecar containers GA" | Tier 3 (logo on #326CE5) → Tier 4 | Kubernetes is a single named brand, no event photo applies. |
| "CISA adds CVE-2026-31431 to KEV" | Tier 1 (CISA-published advisory image, public domain) → Tier 4 | Government source, public domain. |
| "Specsmaxxing — overcoming AI psychosis" (HN essay) | Tier 4 | Conceptual, no specific subject. |
| "EU AI Act enters phase two" | Tier 1 (EU Commission press image) → Tier 4 | EU press images are usually CC-BY. |

## The legality test in plain English

Before recording a Tier 1/2 image, you must be able to answer **all** of:
1. **Who owns it?** (Name the entity.)
2. **What license?** (Quote the relevant sentence.)
3. **What does the license require?** (Attribution string? Link back? Notice of changes?)
4. **Are we compliant?** (Will the consumer's surface include the attribution?)

If you can't answer all four cleanly, drop to the next tier. Falling through to Tier 4 is **never wrong**.
